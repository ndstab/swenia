Claude Fable 5 released: Anthropic’s strongest model yet, with significant safety-filter caveats

Why it matters

A flagship capability jump from a leading lab — the kind of release the entire field will discuss, with benchmark scores and post-training safety measures that directly affect how researchers and engineers can use the model.

Anthropic released Claude Fable 5 publicly, described as a major leap on benchmarks and priced at roughly 2× current Opus models, less than GPT-5.5 Pro. The model was reportedly held back 2+ months post-training. Notably, some prompts are downgraded to Opus 4.8 via safety filters without user disclosure. No single breakthrough (e.g. inference-time scaling, RL) is publicly credited; gains are attributed to full-stack advances.
